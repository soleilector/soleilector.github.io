            <h3 style="color:white;">Flourish native habitats</h3>
            <p>
                Ecosystems have developed over time as a careful balancing act played by its participants.As a part of this global ecosystem, 
                 human activities play a substantial role in the outlook of other species. The prevention of land redevelopment and consequent
                 desertification not only benefits chinook salmon, but also our local farmers and food supply.
            </p>
        </div>
    </div>
    <!-- Free the streams -->
    <div class="info_box wide">
        <div class="text" style="color:white;">
            <h3 style="color:white;">Free the streams</h3>
            <p>
                Where once water naturally and freely flowed, many barriers have been erected to divert and culvert water for human use to our great benefit.
                 Unfortunately, these actions often mean the demise of both other species and, sometimes, entire food webs.
                 Today, human have developed technological innovations that replace the need for diversion, enabling both humans and animals to fluorish without detriment to one another.
            </p>
        </div>

            <h3 style="color:white;">Reduce invasive species</h3>
            <p>
                Species that lack their natural predators and other stressors that dampen the growth of their population become abundants beyond the limits
                 that which can sustain them. This can lead to the extinction of their newfound prey, creating a butterfly effect against those
                 that depend on that prey to survive. Never release a pet into the wild.
            </p>
        </div>
    </div>
    <!-- Educate others -->

            <p>
                Let others know the importance that chinook salmon play. Don't worry, helping conservation efforts doesn't require you to become a messiah in the name of saving salmon.
                 In a world of rapidly-advancing technology, simply liking and sharing information on the importance of these salmon through your social media can make a big difference.
                 Go further by donating to conservation efforts for as little as a a few cents.
