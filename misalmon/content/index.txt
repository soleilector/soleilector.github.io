<h3>about the king...</h3>
<p style='margin:auto;text-align:center;'>An important keystone species from which millions of humans, and
    thousands of other numerous species, find sustenance and prosperity. This ecological treasure is now an endangered species, with only 473,000 adult salmon left in the entire world (US EPA).</p>
<h3 style='text-align:center;width:90%;margin:auto;'>Fast Facts</h3>
<div class='info_box'> <!-- BEGIN info box -->
    <img src='{images}lifecycle.webp' style='width:25%;height:50%;'/>
    <p> <!-- Information text -->
        <i>The largest of all salmon species...</i><br>
        <b>Other names:</b> King salmon, Black mouth salmon<br>
        <b>Prey:</b> Other fish, amphipods, crustaceans, insects<br>
        <b>Predators:</b> Orcas, sealons, sharks, seals, birds, other fish<br>
        <b>Length:</b> 3 to 4.9 feet<br>
        <b>Weight:</b> 3 to 130 lbs<br>
        <b>Habitat:</b> Freshwater streams, estuaries, wetlands, oceans<br><br>
        <a href='lifecycle.html'><button>Lifecycle</button></a>
    </p>
</div> <!-- END info box -->

        Because salmon die after spawning, their carcasses also provide abundant food and nutrients to plants and animals, including tiny aquatic insects and other invertebrates that in turn provide food for other animals.<br>
